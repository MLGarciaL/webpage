Notes, Solved Papers & Demonstrations

This section is currently under development. Below is a only sample for reference:

When reading papers or studying theorems in class, it is common to take results for granted. My approach, however, follows a process I call “not trusting the paper-solution”: I go through each result carefully and, whenever a formal demonstration is not provided, I derive it myself. While this approach is more time-consuming than accepting results at face value, it allows for a deeper understanding of the mechanisms behind them and builds the tools to tackle future derivations independently.

This section is dedicated to my work on solving and understanding economic papers. Its purpose is twofold: to signal my abilities and to provide positive externalities for others and for myself, allowing readers to make recommendations or comments on my demonstrations. Clarification: as this is the work of a master–undergraduate student, errors may be present.

For each paper, I summarize the central question, outline the key mechanisms, provide intuitive explanations, and explain why it matters. Additionally, I include step-by-step derivations and, whenever possible, graphical insights to illustrate the logic of the arguments. For theorems, I present both the formal demonstration and the underlying intuition, complemented with graphical interpretations when appropriate.
